Okonomiyaki

A Savory Pancake from Japan

On the recommendation of a good friend, I tried Okonomiyaki the first time I visited Japan. First in Osaka, an eclectic culinary metropolis, where I fell in love with the dish, I was informed that there are various regional styles of Okonomiyaki to try. So I made it my mission to try this dish everywhere I went. Variations of styles and ingredients are an important part of what makes this dish so special.

The name "Okonomiyaki" translates to "Grilled as you like it", and as the name implies, this recipe leaves a lot of room for experimentation and expression to serve the unique tastes of people enjoying the food. In this recipe I will focus on Osaka style Okonomiyaki, but I encourage you to try all the regional recipes available.

Ingredients

  • 100g Flour
  • 150ml Water
  • 1 Egg
  • 1/8 Cabbage
  • 1/2 Green Onion
  • 1 block Mochi (Optional)
  • 1/2 Japanese Yam or Taro
  • Okonomi Sauce
  • Kewpie Mayo
  • Your favorite protein

Steps

  1. Shred or chop cabbage into a large mixing bowl
  2. Grind Japanese Yam into a paste and add to the mixing bowl
  3. Cut protein to bite size and add to mixing bowl
  4. Preheat a griddle or frying pan on medium heat
  5. Mix the contents of the bowl with water, flour, and egg
  6. Pour the mixed ingredients onto the pan and cook both sides for 6 minutes
  7. Cover with Okonomi Sauce, Kewpie Mayo, and Green Onions before serving
